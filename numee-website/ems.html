EMS body sculpting

Build muscle and burn fat simultaneously — no gym, no surgery, no downtime.

What Is EMS Body Sculpting?

EMS (Electromagnetic Muscle Stimulation) body sculpting is a non-invasive treatment that uses high-intensity focused electromagnetic energy to induce powerful muscle contractions — far beyond what you can achieve voluntarily through exercise. Each 30-minute session triggers approximately 20,000 supramaximal contractions, equivalent to performing 20,000 sit-ups or squats.

The result? Increased muscle mass and accelerated fat burning — simultaneously, without breaking a sweat.

How Does It Work?

An applicator is placed directly on the target area. The electromagnetic energy penetrates through the skin and fat layer to stimulate the underlying muscles at an intensity that is impossible to achieve through regular exercise alone. This causes rapid, repeated muscle contractions that force the muscle tissue to adapt and remodel — building new muscle fibres while also breaking down surrounding fat cells through a process called apoptosis.

Sessions are 30 minutes per area. Most clients notice results after 2–4 sessions, with optimal results typically achieved after 4–6 sessions spaced a few days apart.

Which Areas Can Be Treated?

  • Abdomen (abs and core)
  • Glutes (buttocks lifting and shaping)
  • Arms (biceps and triceps)
  • Thighs (inner and outer)
  • Calves

What Results Can I Expect?

Clinical studies show an average of 16% increase in muscle mass and 19% reduction in fat in the treated area after a recommended course of treatments. Results continue to improve for several weeks after your final session as the body completes its natural remodelling process.

EMS works best alongside a healthy diet and active lifestyle, but many clients are amazed by how effectively it targets areas that stubbornly resist traditional exercise.

Is There Any Downtime?

No downtime. You may feel some muscle soreness in the days following your session — similar to how you'd feel after a very intense workout — but this is a normal and positive sign that the muscles are responding. You can resume all normal activities immediately.
